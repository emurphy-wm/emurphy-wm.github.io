Indigenous Populations of Canada

Purpose

This webpage aims to visualize informations about Canada's indigenous population. In particular, it aims to answer where indigenous populations are located and patterns in mental health and social ecoomic status among their communities. It uses data from Canada's 2021 census and the info-base Health Inequalities tool.

Indigenous People are a Small Part of the Overall Canadian Population

Nunavut, Northwest Territories, and Yukon Have the Largest Indigenous Populations

Nunavut features a high concentration of Inuit people. Along with the Northwest territory, it is one of the only Canadian territories to have a non indigenous majority. The Northwest territories and Yukon also featuring large indigenous populations, but thee populations have greater numbers of First Nations people.
